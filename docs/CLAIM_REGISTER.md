# Claim register

Maturity is fixed at this release: six claims have medium support, two have initial support and four remain design-stage. “Support” combines literature/theory and executable mechanism evidence; it is not a real-world product-effect grade.

| ID | Claim | Maturity | Current executable evidence | What would falsify or downgrade it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C01 | The core problem includes evidence, path, action, evaluation and operating discontinuities | Medium | architecture maps each discontinuity to a separate contract and failure | field study finds the discontinuities do not affect decisions or workflow |
| C02 | Static profiles should become sourced, time-aware, versioned evidence states | Medium | decay, reliability, duplicate, missing and context tests | no improvement over a simple ledger or users misread states as traits |
| C03 | Students need view, dispute, correction and withdrawal rights | Initial | consent/dispute schemas and withdrawal blocking | usability/governance cannot make rights understandable or operational |
| C04 | Planning should retain switchable, reversible multi-path states | Medium | temporal rules, uncertainty, Pareto and invalid-rule tests | system cannot replay, switch or rollback paths in real workflow |
| C05 | Advice should become minimum actions that produce information | Medium | Safe-VOI value, hard gates and dominance tests | no advantage over lowest-burden-first in preregistered outcomes |
| C06 | Growth should be evaluated relative to starting conditions with uncertainty | Medium | VA formula, invariance/sample/width stops and typed boundary | direction flips across reasonable reference/model specifications |
| C07 | Enterprise service effect must be separated from student growth | Design stage | ITT/AIPW target-trial and positivity/version tests | no ethical comparator or credible identification can be implemented |
| C08 | Multi-agent reliability requires permissions, conflicts, logs and human escalation | Initial | capability tests, deterministic orchestration and audit chain | no reliability or operating benefit versus a simpler single workflow |
| C09 | Feishu/GrowthOps can turn AI output into operational queues and learning | Design stage | system emits reason codes/review tickets; no production connector | Feishu workflow cannot meet permission, audit or student-channel needs |
| C10 | A transparent MVP can test key assumptions at low cost | Medium | runnable package, API, synthetic bundle, red team and CI | usability/no-harm thresholds fail or adviser workload is excessive |
| C11 | The platform can improve adviser efficiency, lifecycle conversion and evidence for schools | Design stage | measurable KPI definitions only; no internal data | pilot finds no efficiency/retention gain or unacceptable commercial pressure |
| C12 | Long-term defensibility comes from combined longitudinal evidence, path knowledge, evaluation and governance | Design stage | versioned interfaces and governance architecture only | components are easily replaced without performance or workflow loss |

## Allowed release statement

“v0.1.0 is a tested, synthetic-only reference implementation of the T³-C² coupling and its failure gates.”

## Prohibited release statement

“The platform has proven that it improves student outcomes, accurately predicts careers, or establishes the causal value of enterprise services.”
